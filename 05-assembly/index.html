Cura

A slow-fashion pop-up built on a single word, care.

Scroll

Everything about Cura returns to one word, care. For the materials, the craft behind them, and the environment that yields them.

A retail edit and a hands-on making studio, side by side, inside Carriageworks for Sydney Festival. Slow down, touch and notice natural materials, and leave with something you made yourself. Arrive a browser, leave a maker.

The site

A 19th-century brick rail workshop.

Exposed brick, repeating iron-framed gabled bays, raw industrial volume. That rawness set an earthy, grounded material direction, and the inner-city, values-driven Festival crowd made slow fashion the natural fit.

Two artists shaped it

Janet Laurence gave the why: glass, botanicals and veils, nature as care. Kengo Kuma gave the how: layered bamboo and soft, filtered light.

The name

Cura, Latin for care.

The customer

Slow-fashion, conscious-living; largely female, 25 to 45, inner-city, tactile.

The response

Curved 80 m² plan: two zones, two entries, four senses, zero landfill.

Two interlocking zones

The Mood and Offering

Zone 01 · Retail edit, outer ring

Garments, textiles and lifestyle pieces. Curved rails, a travertine-look bench, recycled-timber shelves, cream limewash walls, sheer linen drapes, backlit herbarium panels and a change room.

Zone 02 · Personalisation studio, inner

Hands-on. Customise raw linen with botanical printing and natural dyes, then take it home. A cashier counter, a rammed-earth bar with FSC spotted-gum top, raw stools, glazed botanical displays.

The sensory layer

Four senses engaged

S · 01

Sight

Warm rammed-earth tones and sandy ochre; light through the bamboo screens and linen drapes; the dried botanical canopy overhead; backlit herbarium panels and glazed botanical displays.

S · 02

Touch

Rammed-earth walls, the FSC spotted-gum bench top, raw-linen garments and the travertine-look plinth, surfaces you want to run a hand over.

S · 03

Smell

Raw earth and clay, the natural dyes working during the workshop, dried botanicals, and FSC timber.

S · 04

Sound

Quiet, nature-layered background music carried softly across both zones.

The plan

An organic curve, two zones interlocked.

80 m², a soft boundary, entry from either side. Retail edit as the outer ring, the studio at the heart. The existing polished concrete stays.
