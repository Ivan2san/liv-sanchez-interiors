# Workflow overlay: SDS09 Small Commercial Project (Sydney Festival pop-up)

Runs on top of the base studio. Adds what SDS09 needs and maps AI tools onto the AutoCAD and SketchUp deliverables. Tool swaps are a concept aid; confirm the educator accepts them, or do the assessed plan and 3D by hand and use AI only for the exhibit.

## New stages (in order)

**0. Research** (assessed, interim critique). Four strands: client and event (Sydney Festival, three weeks in January), site (Carriageworks, heritage iron-framed brick bays, Tonkin Zulaikha Greer adaptive reuse), one influential artist or designer chosen from the six (Sejima, Debris, Lawrence, Do Ho Suh, Eliasson, Kuma) after researching all six, and target customers. Output a research board image plus notes that the concept must trace back to.

**Brief** (base, extended). Capture the two required zones (a commercial zone and a linked interactive zone), the minimum-three-senses requirement, sustainability as central, and the NCC constraints (Class 6 food/beverage, egress, accessibility, balustrades over 1m, flammability index, AS 4674-2004 for food prep).

**1b. Concept development** (base). Form study, hero materials board, section sketch, now anchored to the chosen artist's influence and stating which three senses the space engages.

**Rendered floor plan** (replaces AutoCAD + Photoshop). Generate with Nano Banana Pro plan-to-render from a hand sketch or massing, fitted to a Carriageworks bay. Label zones, circulation (0.8 to 1.0m between tables), egress and access. Assessor expects a measured, code-aware plan, so keep dimensions honest.

**3D perspectives** (replaces SketchUp). Nano Banana Pro stills plus Kling clips for the key views, showing how the concept reads and how it shapes the customer journey through both zones.

**Sample board with code and legend** (assessed). Hero materials composition plus a coded legend: each material keyed to where it appears in the plan, with its sustainability credential (renewable, recycled, non-toxic, reusable or biodegradable at dismantle). This is the MSFID colour-and-finishes core, marked.

**Schedule** (assessed). Furniture, lighting and finishes schedule. Commercial-grade furniture only, correct dims (chair seat 420 to 450mm, bar stool 750mm at 1050mm bar, banquette 450mm at 720mm table), layered lighting (ambient, accent, direct, decorative), day-versus-night scheme.

**Presentation** (replaces base assembly; assessed verbal). Build the web exhibit to follow the fixed SDS09 structure in order: introduce and thank the client, recap the brief and aim, recap the research, recap the concept and its relevance, walk the rendered plan highlighting features, show the 3D perspectives and customer impact, present the sample board with sustainability rationale, conclude against the client's requirements (not personal taste). The exhibit is the visual backbone she presents from verbally.

**Self-evaluation** (assessed). Successes, what to change to communicate the concept better, challenges translating concept to space, and how to improve concept development next time.

## AI tool map
- Rendered plan, sample board, perspectives, research board: Nano Banana Pro (Higgsfield) or Google Gemini if out of credits, same prompts.
- Motion perspectives and the dawn/dusk or sensory shift: Kling.
- Multi-sensory and sustainability are argument, not image: they live in the concept copy, the sample-board legend, and the spoken script, not in a render.
