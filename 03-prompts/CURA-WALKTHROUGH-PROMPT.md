# CURA walkthrough, 15s photoreal (for Higgsfield)

Design guidance: the SketchUp perspectives and elevations (curved organic plan, bamboo batten
screen ring, glazed botanical cylinder, central personalisation counter, travertine plinths,
curved garment rails). Environment and mood: the mood board, offering and concept board, set
inside the Carriageworks heritage bay. Photoreal and warm, like the reference clip.

## Settings
- Model: Kling 3.0, multi-shot, 15 seconds, 16:9, sound off.
- Start frame (recommended): the photoreal exterior-approach still
  (`images/walkthrough-start-exterior.png`), so the whole clip locks to the photoreal look.
- Optional references if the model accepts them: mood-board.png, offering.png, concept-board.png.

## Prompt (paste this)

```
A slow, continuous 15-second photorealistic architectural walkthrough of CURA, a slow-fashion
pop-up shop set inside a heritage Carriageworks rail shed: exposed grey brick walls, repeating
iron-framed gabled trusses overhead, tall arched windows, a polished concrete floor, soft
filtered daylight raking through. The camera begins outside the pop-up, gliding slowly forward
across the hall toward a curved freestanding volume wrapped in a vertical bamboo batten screen,
then passes through an opening in the screen into the retail edit: curved blackened-steel
garment rails hung with naturally dyed and botanically printed linen garments in ochre,
terracotta, sage and cream, round travertine display plinths stacked with folded linen, and a
softly glowing glazed botanical display cylinder filled with greenery. The camera continues
inward and pushes gently toward the central personalisation counter, an organic curved bench
with a layered rammed-earth base in ochre and terracotta strata and a warm raw-timber top, raw
timber stools, handmade ceramic vessels, and dried botanicals suspended overhead. Then the
camera lifts and eases back into a slow rising aerial that turns to look down over the whole
curved pop-up, the bamboo screen ring, the counter and the botanicals within the brick bay.
Warm earthy palette of rammed earth, cream limewash, raw timber, bamboo, stone, raw and
naturally dyed linen and living botanicals, all matte, natural and tactile. Photorealistic
architectural visualisation, editorial and calm, true matte colour and fine material texture,
soft natural daylight, shallow depth of field, slow constant camera speed, single flowing take.
No people, no text, no logos, no warping, no melting, no distortion, no camera shake.
```

## Tips
- If 15s drifts or warps, split into two 8s halves (approach plus retail, then studio plus
  aerial) and join them, or drop to 10s.
- Keep the start frame photoreal; a SketchUp start frame pulls the whole clip back to the
  SketchUp look.
- One reroll budget: bank a clean take before trying variations.
