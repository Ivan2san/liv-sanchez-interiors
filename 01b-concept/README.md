# Concept development

The thinking behind the design, before the renders. This is what separates a polished exhibit from a styled one (the substance check), and what an assessor marks for under BSBCRT512.

Produce three artefacts here, drawn from the brief:
- A form study or concept model (a hand-formed maquette, a study object).
- A hero materials board (physical samples with depth and shadow, not a flat swatch grid).
- A massing or section sketch (hand-drawn, showing the spatial and light idea).

Prompts for all three are in 03-prompts/PROMPT-LIBRARY.md (image section). Generate in Higgsfield (Nano Banana Pro) or, if out of credits, in Google Gemini with the same prompts.

These lead the final exhibit, above the drawings and scenes, so the page reads origin to render.
